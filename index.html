GIS SuperPro Helps Stop Lead Posioning in Flint, Michigan!!!

As a GIS superhero committed to safeguarding the well-being of Flint, Michigan, I tackled the pressing issue of identifying those most vulnerable to drinking poisonous tap water. Armed with the invaluable data provided, I embarked on a mission to pinpoint the at-risk populations. By integrating information from multiple sources, including the Toxic Release Inventory (TRI) facilities emitting over 10,000 tons of water pollution, the proximity of residential areas to rivers, and the presence of lead service lines within a .2-mile radius, I meticulously delineated the geographic regions under threat. I accurately mapped out the vulnerable zones. Utilizing only the 'lead' category to define lead service lines, I ensured precision in my analysis. Employing dissolving all types of buffers, I created comprehensive buffers around each identified risk factor, thus formulating a holistic approach to addressing the problem. This solution is significant not only because it can identify the most at-risk populations but also because it can guide targeted interventions and resource allocation. By proactively identifying areas susceptible to contaminated water, we can implement preventive measures, offer support to affected communities, and work towards ensuring the safety and well-being of all residents. This endeavor underscores the essence of proactive and data-driven decision-making in safeguarding public health and fostering resilience in the face of environmental challenges.
